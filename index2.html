Recap: Overdrafts Level 2

Here’s a reminder of what you learnt at Level 2:
  • An overdraft is a form of borrowing from your bank.
  • If you have less than £0 in your account, you are overdrawn.
  • Banks charge interest on the amount you are overdrawn, and the interest rate is high.
  • Overdrafts can be arranged or unarranged.
  • Overdrafts are meant for short-term or emergency use.
  • You must be aged 18 or over to have an overdraft.

Lisa is overdrawn by £400. Every week that she is overdrawn, the bank charges her interest of 1%.

To find 1% of something, you can divide it by 100. Here, the maths is 400 ÷ 100 = 4.

So 1% of £400 is £4.

On the first week Lisa is overdrawn, the bank will add £4 to what she must pay back.

You’ve just seen how 1% interest per week quickly adds up. Banks will tell you interest rates per year (the annual rate). But they calculate interest per day you are overdrawn.

Banks usually charge around 40% interest on overdrafts per year (annually). Banks call this EAR: Equivalent Annual Rate.
